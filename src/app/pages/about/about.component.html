Hi, I'm Jonathan

Software engineer based in Minnesota who's been building web applications for the past nine years. What started as curiosity about how software works has turned into a career centered around solving problems with code.

My Journey

I graduated from Metropolitan State University with a Computer Science degree and jumped straight into building applications with .NET and SQL Server. Over the years, I've worked at everything from small companies where I wore multiple hats to larger organizations with established processes and enterprise-scale challenges.

My experience has taken me through different industries - from insurance and manufacturing to payroll and marketing. Each environment taught me something different about how software fits into business operations.

What Drives Me

I genuinely enjoy the problem-solving aspect of development. Every project brings unique constraints and requirements, and finding elegant solutions within those boundaries keeps the work interesting.

Beyond the technical challenges, I find great value in mentoring other developers. There's something rewarding about helping someone work through a difficult concept or watching them grow in their skills.

Current Focus

These days, I spend most of my time building full-stack applications with .NET and Angular. I've worked on interesting challenges like automating e-filing systems, building SSO solutions, and migrating legacy applications to modern frameworks.

I'm particularly drawn to system architecture and how applications scale - designing solutions that handle growth gracefully, whether that's more users, data, or complex business requirements.

Why CodeDaily

I started this blog to share practical lessons from real projects including actual trade-offs and decisions that come up when building software in business environments, along with the latest new and updates in software developement.
